# Narrative Overview

This is the narrative north star for *Tales from Ulcantron: Purity Wars*. Read this first.

## High Concept

*Purity Wars* is a mythic political fantasy RPG set in the kingdom of Azzentia, where long-brewing tension between advocates of class purity and supporters of class mixing erupts into civil war after the royal family fractures from within.

## One-Sentence Premise

Brewing tension between those who believe class should remain pure and those who believe classes should be free to mix spills into open war when the princess of Azzentia publicly rejects the kingdom's purity doctrine.

## Narrative Overview

In Azzentia, class is more than a profession or talent: it is identity, status, duty, and social order. For generations, the kingdom has lived under a rigid belief that each person should follow a single, pure path — whether inherited, chosen, or spiritually affirmed. A Knight may deepen into a Holy Knight; a Mage may refine into an Enchanted Mage; specialisation is celebrated. Mixing is not. To stand between two classes is to dilute both, and to dilute oneself is, by the prevailing doctrine, to dilute the kingdom.

The story opens just before that doctrine breaks. The royal family of Azzentia carries the contradiction at its centre. The King is a public guarantor of purity. The Queen sits between her crown and her daughter. The Princess is a living counterexample to the ideology her family is sworn to uphold — and she is no longer willing to be quiet about it.

When the fracture goes public, neutrality stops being an option. The kingdom takes sides. The war that follows is mythic in scale but intimate in its causes. The broad outcome of the war is consistent across the player's choices; what changes is *whose* story they live, *what* they witness, and *what* they carry forward.

## Series Framing

The full series is **Tales from Ulcantron**. *Purity Wars* is one episode set within Azzentia, a single kingdom in the wider world. Future Tales episodes can move to other regions of Ulcantron without re-justifying the world rules.

## Tone

Heroic and political. The voice is elevated, mythic, romantic, and politically charged, while still allowing warmth, humour, whimsy, and wonder in the world itself. The story should feel like a legend being lived, not a documentary, even as it reflects recognisable real-world tensions.

## Intended Player Experience

Players should feel drawn into a dramatic and emotionally legible conflict that mirrors real social divisions through fantasy metaphor. The narrative should provoke reflection, relatability, and humility while still delivering the excitement, romance, and momentum of a playable heroic story. The intended ending is ultimately hopeful: a movement away from division and toward coexistence, even after violence and loss have already shaped what comes next.

## Player Framing

The player begins as a young figure close enough to power to witness the royal split from inside the court — not yet a hero in the saga sense, not yet free of it either. Personalisation in the prologue is light (e.g. presented gender). Full character creation is deferred to the post-prologue character-creation scene, where the choice of Pure or Mixed identity becomes the pivot the rest of the game runs on.

## Thematic Anchors

These should remain visible in writing, scene framing, and quest design:

1. **Identity is not a metaphor.** Class is mechanical, social, and spiritual all at once. Mechanics and theme point at the same thing.
2. **The first violence is private.** Public war is downstream of a family that could not hold itself together.
3. **The ideology is genuinely attractive.** Purity offers belonging, predictability, and pride. The story falls flat if it caricatures the people who believe in it.
4. **The same outcome, different witnesses.** History does not change much by route; what changes is the player's relationship to the cost.

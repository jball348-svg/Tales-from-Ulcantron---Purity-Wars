# Dialogue Style Guide

## Status

**Stub.** Reserved for the working voice rules every line of dialogue should pass. The first concrete dialogue work lives in `docs/prologue/`; whatever rules emerge from polishing that pass should be lifted up into this guide.

## What This Doc Will Contain

- **Tonal register.** The "elevated but human" rule from the narrative overview, with specific examples of acceptable and unacceptable phrasings.
- **What dialogue is for.** Reveal character and stakes; don't lecture the world. Dialogue should never carry pure exposition that could be read off a sign.
- **Faction voices.** Pure-faction characters should not all sound the same; Mixed-faction characters should not all sound the same. Voice variance per institution (temple vs. knight order vs. academy vs. rural).
- **Class-aware speech.** Multiclass adults should sometimes refer to their dual identity in ways that read naturally — not always defensive, not always proud. Pure adults should sometimes refer to purity in ways that are not slogans.
- **Per-character voice notes.** Short anchors for the King (controlled, paternal, doctrinally fluent), the Queen (precise, measured, withholding), the Princess (younger than her position, increasingly direct), and the player's default narrator voice.
- **NPC voice patterns** for civilians, soldiers, merchants, priests, and rural workers, kept short enough to be useful at write-time.
- **Non-negotiables.** Lines never to write (modern slang, knowing winks, fourth-wall jokes, puns on the title).

## Authoring Rules When Filled In

- Every voice rule needs at least one before/after example. Rules without examples don't help writers.
- Keep this doc compact. If it becomes a textbook, nobody reads it.
- Update from real prologue dialogue, not from theoretical preference.

# Factions

## Status

**Stub.** Reserved for the named factions, their leaders, their territories, their grievances, and the specific institutions they control. The world-rule layer is in `02_world_setting.md`; the character layer is in `05_characters.md`. Faction granularity belongs here.

## What This Doc Will Contain

- The two main alignments — **Pure** and **Mixed** — broken down into their actual constituent factions, not treated as monoliths. There should be at least two named Pure factions (e.g. doctrinal vs. pragmatic) and at least two named Mixed factions (e.g. reformist vs. revolutionary).
- The institutions each faction effectively controls (a knight order, a temple, an academy, a trade guild, a militia network).
- The eight-way regional division of Azzentia from `02_world_setting.md`, with each "slice" tagged for which faction it leans toward at the outbreak of the war and which way it shifts over Act II.
- Neutral and out-of-kingdom actors — the foreign embassies, the merchant companies that stay neutral for as long as they can, the rural pockets that try to opt out.

## Authoring Rules When Filled In

- No faction should be entirely good or entirely bad. The Pure factions need believers, not bigots-only. The Mixed factions need cynics, opportunists, and the genuinely afraid alongside the heroes.
- Faction politics should be readable inside a single quest. If understanding why a quest matters requires reading the whole codex, the writing is doing too much in the wrong place.
- Faction territory and the eight-region map should agree with whatever map asset eventually ships. Until that asset exists, name the regions but leave the precise borders open.

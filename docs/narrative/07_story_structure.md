# Story Structure

The campaign of *Purity Wars* runs in three large movements. The broad outcome of the war does not change between routes. What changes is whose perspective the player carries through the story, what they witness firsthand, and what they take into the post-war epilogue.

## Three-Act Frame

### Act I — Pre-War

- **Setting:** the everyday Azzentia the war will eventually end. Civic life is recognisable. The Rite of Naming, the trade fairs, the temple ceremonies, the regional politics, the small daily inconveniences of multiclass adults.
- **Purpose:** earn the player's affection for the world *before* the war takes it apart. Establish the doctrine as something many people genuinely believe, and the contradiction at the centre of the royal family.
- **Endpoint:** the public moment of the princess refusing to hide. Doctrine cannot absorb it. Sides must be chosen.

The **prologue** ("The Day of Choosing") sits inside Act I as the playable opening, before character creation. See `docs/prologue/`.

### Act II — During-War

- **Setting:** the kingdom at war with itself. Pure-faction strongholds, mixed-faction strongholds, contested towns, neutral pockets that are quietly disappearing.
- **Purpose:** show the cost. Both sides have moral compromises. Both sides have humanity. The player's choice of Pure or Mixed alignment changes which compromises they participate in and which they are forced to watch.
- **Endpoint:** the war's decisive event. The same broad outcome lands across all routes — but the player has been positioned by their choices to read it differently.

### Act III — Post-War

- **Setting:** the kingdom afterward. Restoration is partial, transformation is real, grief is the dominant ambient emotion.
- **Purpose:** the hopeful ending. Coexistence is possible, but it has been bought, and the price is not undone. The player's choices shape the *texture* of the new Azzentia, not the fact of it.
- **Endpoint:** an exit beat that opens space for future *Tales from Ulcantron* episodes elsewhere in the world.

## Route Architecture

The two main alignments — **Pure** and **Mixed** — are committed at the post-prologue character-creation scene. From there, the routes share most of the same locations and most of the same major events, but:

- **Allegiance changes** access to certain quests, factions, and named characters
- **Perspective changes** how the same event reads — whose mouths the words come out of, what is celebrated, what is mourned
- **Relationships change** who survives, who breaks, who the player ends the game beside
- **Interpretation changes** what the post-war restoration *means* in the player's voice

The intent is not to deliver two different games. It is to deliver one tragedy from two different vantage points — both of which leave the player having lost something they could see clearly.

## Why The Outcome Is Fixed

A branching outcome would dilute the central tragedy. The political point of the story is not "you can win the war for the right side." It is that the war happens, that both sides pay, and that the world afterward is shaped less by which side won and more by what the people who lived through it choose to do next.

## Beats That Should Stay Consistent Across Routes

- The royal-family fracture goes public.
- The kingdom polarises.
- Open war begins (a specific named flashpoint, to be defined).
- A major personal loss for the player around the midpoint.
- A decisive battle near the end of Act II.
- Restoration begins in Act III, imperfect but real.

## Beats That Are Free To Vary

- Which named NPCs the player travels with.
- Which towns the player saves or fails to save.
- Which institutions the player ends up reforming, defending, or watching collapse.
- The specific moment of the player's clearest personal moral test.
- Which characters appear in the epilogue beats.

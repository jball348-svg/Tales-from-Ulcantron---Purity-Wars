# BS-02 — Captain of the Royal Guard Battle Pose — v01 source prompt

Sent to **Google Gemini** (Fast model, gemini.google.com) to produce `assets/proprietary/production/sprites/captain_royal_guard_battle_v01.png`.

This is the first asset generated under the pivoted spec: **single-pose painted half-body renders instead of 4×3 pixel-art sprite sheets**. Both ChatGPT-free and Gemini-free were unable to produce engine-ready 4×3 sprite sheets in the 2026-05-11 session (both produced painted character art arranged in a grid, not pixel-art 64×64 frames). The pivot is to lean into what consumer text-to-image models DO well — high-quality single-pose painted characters — and use them as `battle` / `cutscene` sprite textures in Godot. Map-walking sprites for the prologue cast continue to use the LPC bookstore placeholder until a sprite-sheet-specialised pipeline is wired up.

Constituent blocks: Style Anchor + a modified Per-Category B (painterly instead of pixel-art; facing-left orientation for enemies) + per-asset BS-02 (from `docs/asset_generation_prompts.md`).

---

## Full prompt as sent

```
Visual style anchor for the game "Tales from Ulcantron: Purity Wars".

Setting: a mythic political fantasy kingdom called Azzentia. Tone: ceremonial, pre-storm, beautiful, slightly oppressive. Reference visual: a painted key-art piece showing a caped sword-bearing figure on a cliff before a violet-lit fortress under an eclipsed sun.

Palette anchor: deep violet, near-black, ember orange highlights, candlelight gold, parchment cream, restrained teal accents. Avoid pure-saturation reds and pure-saturation greens. No neon. No modern brand colors.

Lighting: directional and candlelit, with strong silhouette readability against muted backgrounds. Subjects should read at small sizes.

Cultural register: high fantasy with a JRPG sensibility, sitting between Final Fantasy and Dungeons & Dragons. Magik (with a 'k') is woven into daily life — recognizable and regulated, not marvelled at. Ceremonial and civic spaces matter. No modern technology, no modern clothing, no fourth-wall winks.

Composition rules: clean focal silhouette; quiet negative space; no frames, watermarks, captions, signatures, or text labels of any kind unless explicitly requested. Do not overlay UI mockups, menus, or button shapes onto the artwork unless the prompt asks for them.

The asset to generate is described below.

Category: side-on battle sprite for use against a backdrop in turn-based combat.

Output a single PNG with a fully transparent background.

Painterly illustration style — the same medium and finish as the title key art, not pixel art. Render as a full-body or three-quarter-body character render, no environment behind them. Strong directional candlelight, palette anchored as above.

Single static pose, character facing left (this character is the enemy in the battle camera — the player attendant is on the screen left, the enemy Captain on the right, so the Captain faces back toward the player). Character vertically centered in the frame, with their feet near the bottom edge. Pose should read instantly as the character's combat stance.

The pose and character to render are described below.

Subject: the Captain of the Royal Guard in a battle pose during the unwinnable fight in the chapel ceremony hall. Same identity as the PR-04 portrait — same plate armor, same ceremonial scar, same tired-but-absolute expression.

Pose: drawn longsword held low and ready, weight forward, helm closed. Composed. Nothing about the pose suggests he is straining. The blade catches faint candlelight along its edge.

Visibly larger silhouette than a young court attendant would be — this is a man who has done this work hundreds of times.
```

## Notes

- v01 generated by Google Gemini desktop on 2026-05-11.
- Runtime path: `assets/proprietary/production/sprites/captain_royal_guard_battle_v01.png`.
- Wired into `autoloads/ActorVisuals.gd` as the `ACTOR_CAPTAIN_ROYAL_GUARD.battle` texture entry (type "texture"). Once `BATTLE_KIND_PROLOGUE_INCITING` lands in Battle.gd, this sprite renders as the enemy at `ENEMY_IDLE_POSITION` during the unwinnable fight.
- Pivoted spec note: the original BS-02 spec called for 128×160 pixel art. The pivoted spec produces a larger painted PNG (Gemini default ~896×1200 or similar). Godot will scale it down to `ENEMY_TARGET_HEIGHT = 78` runtime height. The frame size and pixel-art constraint are dropped; the silhouette readability constraint still applies.
